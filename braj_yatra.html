Braj Yatra....

Braj, a mystical region in northern India encompassing the twin cities of Mathura and Vrindavan, is an enchanting tapestry of culture, spirituality, and timeless devotion. This sacred land is forever etched in the annals of Hindu mythology as the divine playground of Lord Krishna. The very air in Braj is believed to be infused with stories of Krishna's childhood antics, his mesmerizing flute melodies, and the eternal love shared with the gopis, especially Radha. Every nook and corner resonates with a sense of devotion that transcends time, where temple bells and the aroma of incense weave a narrative of unwavering faith.

Taste The Nector of Braj Bhumi

Vrindavan, nestled on the banks of the Yamuna River, is a town steeped in spirituality and love, known as the playground of Lord Krishna. With temples like Banke Bihari and Prem Mandir drawing devotees, the evening aarti by the Yamuna Ghat is a mesmerizing experience. Its grand festivals, especially the vibrant Holi celebrations, bring the streets to life. Ashrams and serene ghats offer a peaceful refuge, while traditional markets showcase vibrant handicrafts and sweets. Vrindavan, where the timeless tales of Radha and Krishna come alive, is a captivating blend of devotion, culture, and spirituality, inviting all to partake in its timeless beauty and divine aura.

One District One Product(Sanitary Fittings,Thakur ji's Dresses)

Sanitary fittings such as taps, etc. are produced in the district. These products are made with core mechanism and sand dye casting method. There are as many as 50 micro and small units involved in production of taps in the district. Mathura is a well-known centre for manufacture and supply of sanitary fittings in Uttar Pradesh.
Mathura is referred as religious city. The cottage industries of the city produce attire and adornment of Lord Krishna, necklace used by sages, and adornment sculptors as major products. Demand of these products continues throughout the year. Domestic and abroad devotees buy these goods when they arrive in Mathura. They also bring back these things from here for their friends and family member as Prasad of Lord Shri Krishna.
