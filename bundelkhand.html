Hamao BundelKhand....

Bundelkhand, an enchanting region in central India, unveils a world where time seems to slow down, and the past gracefully merges with the present. Steeped in history and mythology, this land is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, and its temples and forts narrate tales of bygone eras. Orchha Fort stands tall as an architectural masterpiece, while the spiritual town of Chitrakoot offers a serene retreat. Bundelkhand's cultural tapestry is adorned with traditional dance forms, folk music, and a culinary heritage that tantalizes the taste buds. Nature too has left its mark, with the Panna National Park sheltering a diverse array of wildlife.

Let's Explore BundelKhand

Nestled in the heart of India, Bundelkhand is a hidden gem that beckons travelers with its enchanting blend of history, nature, and culture. This timeless land, with its rugged terrain and fertile plains, boasts a tapestry of experiences waiting to be unraveled. Bundelkhand is a treasure trove of history, where ancient forts like Orchha and Jhansi stand as silent witnesses to tales of valor and dynasties long gone. The echoes of battles fought and victories won resound through the mighty walls of these architectural marvels.

One District One Product

  1. Jhansi(Soft-Toys):A stuffed toy or soft toy is a toy with an outer fabric sewn from a textile and then stuffed with a flexible material. The materials used for making these toys include polycloth, nylax cloth, fiber etc. Stuffed toys are made in many different forms, often resembling real animals, legendary creatures, cartoon characters or inanimate objects.
  2. Mahoba(Gaura Stone):Mahoba is known nationwide for its exquisite gaura stone craft. Gaura stone craft is made of radiant white colored stone that is predominantly found in this region. The stone craft holds a very special place in the world of art and craft. Gaura stone has a soft texture. It is cut into several pieces which are then used for making various craft items that are used for ornamental purpose. Artisans from Mahoba are known worldwide for their unique artworks of unmatched specimens of Gaura stone handicraft.
  3. Banda(Shazar Stone Craft):Shazar Stone is obtained from Ken river which flows in the west of Banda district Bundelkhand region. From obtaining the Shazar stone to trimming and shaping it, the craft involves a lot of hard-work and expenditure. This stone is used for making jewellery and other decorative items.
  4. ChitrakootDham(Wooden Toys):Due to being a forest area, there is abundance of wood (raw material) in Chitrakoot. A large number of craftsmen in this district are engaged in making wooden toys. The toys produced here are sent to different districts of the state for sale as well as for fairs and exhibitions.
