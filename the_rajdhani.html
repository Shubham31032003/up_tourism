The Rajdhani....

Lucknow, the capital city of the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is a captivating blend of history, culture, and modernity. Renowned as the "City of Nawabs," it carries an air of timeless elegance and sophistication. Strolling through its streets feels like a journey through history, with its splendid architectural gems such as the Bara Imambara, Chota Imambara, and the resplendent Rumi Darwaza. Beyond its architectural splendors, Lucknow is celebrated for its culinary delights, offering a feast for the senses with its delectable kebabs, biryanis, and sweets.

Explore the City of Nawabs

Lucknow, the City of Nawabs, is a fascinating destination that seamlessly blends the elegance of its historical past with the vigor of modernity. This city, known for its gracious hospitality and cultural richness, unfolds like a treasure chest of experiences waiting to be explored. At the heart of Lucknow's charm is its remarkable architectural heritage. The Bara Imambara, a colossal edifice with its iconic Bhul Bhulaiya (maze), stands as a testament to the city's grandeur during the Nawab era. Chota Imambara, adorned with intricate chandeliers, mirrors, and Islamic calligraphy, showcases the artistic finesse of the bygone era.

One District One Product(Chikankari and Zari Zardozi)

Chikankari is a traditional embroidery style from Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh. It is one of Lucknow's best known craft whereas Zardozi embroidery is beautiful metal embroidery, which once used to embellish the attire of the Kings and the royals in India. Zardozi embroidery work involves making elaborate designs, using gold and silver threads. Chikankari is traditional embroidery done using a needle and several types of threads whereas Zari-Zardozi work is made with golden and shiny sequins and other decorative materials. Chikkankari and Zari-Zardozi work are registered in the G.I. Lucknowi embroidery is popular all over the world. The craft provides employment to a number of artisans.
