The Ram Rajya.....

Ram Rajya, a concept deeply woven into the tapestry of Hindu culture and mythology, finds its symbolic home in the ancient city of Ayodhya. It stands as a testament to an idyllic era characterized by the reign of Lord Rama, a beloved deity and the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu. In the epic Indian poem, the Ramayana, the rule of Lord Rama in Ayodhya is celebrated as an embodiment of virtuous leadership, where justice, righteousness, and moral values formed the bedrock of society. This era is often depicted as a period of equality, compassion, and prosperity, where the king's unwavering commitment to dharma extended to all his subjects, regardless of their social standing. Ayodhya, as the heart of Ram Rajya, continues to be a revered pilgrimage site, attracting devotees and seekers of the ideal society it represents.

Deep Dive in Ayodhya

Ayodhya, a city steeped in history, mythology, and spirituality, is a captivating destination that beckons travelers from around the world. This enchanting city, situated on the banks of the sacred Sarayu River in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, holds a special place in the hearts of millions as the birthplace of Lord Rama. Ayodhya's allure extends far beyond its religious significance, making it a unique and fascinating tourism hub. The city's spiritual aura extends beyond the boundaries of temples and ghat, with the melodious sound of temple bells and the recitation of hymns filling the air. Ayodhya offers a glimpse into the age-old traditions of Hinduism, fostering an environment of peace and introspection. Pilgrims and tourists alike find solace in the serene atmosphere, which encourages contemplation and spiritual rejuvenation.

One District One Product(Jaggery)

Jaggery making in Ayodhya is being carried out traditionally since generations. About 20% of the total agricultural land of the district is used for sugarcane cultivation. The district mainly produces jaggery and jaggery-products like sesame gajak, ladoo, chikki, gud ke ladoo etc. Sugarcane, the raw material required for making jaggery, is present here in abundance.
